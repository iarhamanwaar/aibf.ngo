'''Anwaar Ahmed Bugvi''' ({{lang-ur|ڈاکٹر انوار احمد بگوی}}) is a Pakistani physician, government health adviser, and the founder of the [[Al-Iftikhar Bugvia Foundation]], a non-governmental charitable organization based in [[Bhera]], [[Sargodha District|Sargodha]], [[Punjab, Pakistan|Punjab]].

== Career ==
Bugvi served as Adviser Health (Technical) to the [[Government of Punjab, Pakistan|Government of Punjab]] and was a member of a four-member inquiry committee constituted by the provincial government in 2005 to investigate examination irregularities at the [[Punjab Medical Faculty]].<ref name="dawn2005">{{cite news |title=PMF officials may face axe: Exam scandal |url=https://www.dawn.com/news/156569 |work=[[Dawn (newspaper)|Dawn]] |date=14 September 2005 |access-date=3 May 2026}}</ref> Alongside Prof. Yasmin Lodhi and Muhammad Athar, he produced the initial inquiry report which documented that the 2004 paramedics examinations had been conducted "in gross violation of rules".<ref name="dawn2005" />

He has practised as a general physician in [[Lahore]].

== Al-Iftikhar Bugvia Foundation ==
In 1998 Bugvi founded the Al-Iftikhar Bugvia Foundation (AIBF), a non-governmental and charitable organization registered with the Punjab Charity Commission as a Category (A) Charity (registration no. PB-6976792864708031).<ref name="aibfabout">{{cite web |title=About AIBF |url=https://aibf.ngo/about |publisher=Al-Iftikhar Bugvia Foundation |access-date=3 May 2026}}</ref> The foundation operates programmes in healthcare, education, ration distribution, livelihood support and emergency relief across Bhera, [[Bugga Sharif]], and surrounding villages in [[Sargodha District|Sargodha]] and [[Jhelum District|Jhelum]] districts.<ref name="aibfabout" />

The foundation's activities have included free health camps, monthly medicine supply to the Pakistan Red Crescent Society Hospital in Bhera, sewing centres for women in Maliyar and Bugga, ration drives during [[Ramadan]], and an interest-free micro-loan ([[qard hasan]]) programme for people in debt.<ref name="aibfabout" /> A youth calligraphy competition organised by the foundation in November 2025 at the Zahoor Auditorium in Bhera was reported by the community heritage portal {{lang|en|''Bhera.org''}}.<ref>{{cite web |title=Calligraphy competition by Iftikhar Bugvia Foundation, 18 November 2025 |url=https://bhera.org/a-calligraphy-competition-for-the-young-students-was-organised-for-the-first-time-by-the-iftikhar-bugvia-foundation-on-18-november-2025-at-zahoor-auditorium-bhera-see-on-the-website-bhera-org-un/ |website=Bhera.org |date=20 November 2025 |access-date=3 May 2026}}</ref>

== Family background ==
Bugvi belongs to the [[Bugvi family]] of Bhera, a Punjabi scholarly family historically associated with the [[Sher Shah Suri Mosque|Sher Shah Suri Mosque (Jamia Masjid Bhera)]] and with Dar-ul-Uloom Azizia, an Islamic seminary established at the mosque in 1841.<ref>{{cite web |title=Dar-ul-Uloom Azizia Bhera |url=https://merasargodha.com/dar-ul-uloom-azizia-bhera/ |website=Mera Sargodha |access-date=3 May 2026}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Sher Shah Suri Mosque / Jamia Masjid Bhera |url=https://archaeology.punjab.gov.pk/jame-masjid-bhera |publisher=Directorate General of Archaeology, Government of the Punjab |access-date=3 May 2026}}</ref> Earlier members of the family include Qazi Ahmed-ud-din Bugvi, who rebuilt the Jamia Masjid in 1858, and Qazi Zahoor Ahmed Bugvi, who repaired it in 1926.<ref name="punjabarch">{{cite web |title=Sher Shah Suri Mosque / Jamia Masjid Bhera |url=https://archaeology.punjab.gov.pk/jame-masjid-bhera |publisher=Directorate General of Archaeology, Government of the Punjab |access-date=3 May 2026}}</ref>
