'''Dar-ul-Uloom Azizia''' ({{lang-ur|دارالعلوم عزیزیہ}}) is an [[Islamic education|Islamic seminary]] located at the [[Sher Shah Suri Mosque|Sher Shah Suri Mosque (Jamia Masjid Bhera)]] in [[Bhera]], [[Sargodha District]], [[Punjab, Pakistan|Punjab]], Pakistan. Established by the [[Bugvi family]] in 1841, the institution is among the oldest continuously-operating Islamic schools in the [[Sargodha District|Sargodha]] region and has been the subject of dedicated academic research on its role in disseminating religious knowledge in Punjab during the nineteenth and twentieth centuries.<ref name="ihya">{{cite journal |title=The Development of Dar-ul-Uloom Azizia, Bhera and Its Role in the Dissemination of Knowledge to Society (1929–1975 A.D.): A Research Study |url=https://joqs-uok.com/index.php/ihya/article/view/64 |journal=Iḥyāʾ al-ʿUlūm |publisher=Department of Quran o Sunnah, University of Karachi}}</ref><ref name="merasargodha">{{cite web |title=Dar-ul-Uloom Azizia Bhera |url=https://merasargodha.com/dar-ul-uloom-azizia-bhera/ |website=Mera Sargodha |access-date=3 May 2026}}</ref>

== History ==
The seminary was founded in 1841 at the historic [[Sher Shah Suri Mosque|Sher Shah Suri Mosque]], originally built in 1540 AD during the [[Sur Empire|Sur era]].<ref name="merasargodha" /><ref name="punjabarch">{{cite web |title=Sher Shah Suri Mosque / Jamia Masjid Bhera |url=https://archaeology.punjab.gov.pk/jame-masjid-bhera |publisher=Directorate General of Archaeology, Government of the Punjab |access-date=3 May 2026}}</ref> The Bugvi family — long-standing custodians of the mosque — established the institution to teach the [[Quran]], [[hadith]] and the religious sciences.<ref name="merasargodha" />

In 1858 the mosque was rebuilt by Qazi Ahmed-ud-din Bugvi, who added two halls dedicated to Quran and Hadith study and adjacent boarding houses for students of the seminary; further restoration was carried out by Qazi Zahoor Ahmed Bugvi in 1926.<ref name="punjabarch" />

The institution underwent significant development between 1929 and 1975, a period studied in depth in the {{lang|ar|Iḥyāʾ al-ʿUlūm}} journal of the Department of Quran and Sunnah at the [[University of Karachi]].<ref name="ihya" />

== Activities ==
Alongside its core religious-education function, Dar-ul-Uloom Azizia has historically been associated with:

* the '''Bugvia Library''', an institutional reference library reported to be approximately two centuries old;<ref>{{cite web |title=Islamic Educational Institutions Bhera City and Villages |url=http://bheratehsil.blogspot.com/2017/05/islamic-educational-institutions-bhera.html |website=Bhera Tehsil |date=May 2017 |access-date=3 May 2026}}</ref>
* the monthly Urdu magazine ''Shams-ul-Islam'', founded in 1925;<ref name="merasargodha" />
* the '''Bugvia Marryland Girls Model High School Bhera''', a girls' high school established by the family;<ref>{{cite web |title=Islamic Educational Institutions Bhera City and Villages |url=http://bheratehsil.blogspot.com/2017/05/islamic-educational-institutions-bhera.html |website=Bhera Tehsil |date=May 2017 |access-date=3 May 2026}}</ref>
* free annual eye camps held since 1978;<ref>{{cite web |title=Islamic Educational Institutions Bhera City and Villages |url=http://bheratehsil.blogspot.com/2017/05/islamic-educational-institutions-bhera.html |website=Bhera Tehsil |date=May 2017 |access-date=3 May 2026}}</ref>
* a free dispensary serving local patients.<ref>{{cite web |title=Islamic Educational Institutions Bhera City and Villages |url=http://bheratehsil.blogspot.com/2017/05/islamic-educational-institutions-bhera.html |website=Bhera Tehsil |date=May 2017 |access-date=3 May 2026}}</ref>

== Leadership ==
The institution is in the charge of '''Sahibzada Abrar Ahmed Bugvi''', who also serves as the [[Khatib]] (sermon-giver) of the Sher Shah Suri Mosque.<ref>{{cite web |title=Gates of Bhera |url=https://bhera.org/gates-of-bhera/ |website=Bhera.org |access-date=3 May 2026}}</ref>

== Connection to the Khilafat Movement ==
[[Maulana Zahoor Ahmed Bugvi]], a member of the family active in the [[Khilafat Movement]] of the early 1920s, was associated with the seminary; he was arrested at Sargodha on 15 March 1922 and imprisoned for one and a half years for his role in organising Khilafat processions across Sargodha district.<ref>{{cite journal |title=The Emergence of the Khilafat Movement in Sargodha: Beginning of Agitational Politics and Impacts on the Freedom Movement |url=https://www.gprjournal.com/fulltext/the-emergence-of-the-khilafat-movement-in-sargodha-beginning-of-agitational-politics-and-impacts-on-the-freedom-movement |journal=Global Political Review}}</ref>

== Modern affiliated charity ==
In 1998 [[Anwaar Ahmed Bugvi|Dr Anwaar Ahmed Bugvi]] founded the [[Al-Iftikhar Bugvia Foundation]] to extend the family's tradition of social service into modern healthcare, education and welfare programmes; the foundation operates from the same mosque complex.<ref>{{cite web |title=About AIBF |url=https://aibf.ngo/about |publisher=Al-Iftikhar Bugvia Foundation |access-date=3 May 2026}}</ref>
