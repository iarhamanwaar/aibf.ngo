{{Short description|Pakistani Punjabi scholarly family from Bhera}}

The '''Bugvi family''' (also '''Bugvia''') is a [[Punjab, Pakistan|Punjabi]] [[Sayyid|sayyid]] scholarly family from [[Bhera]], in [[Sargodha District]], [[Punjab, Pakistan|Punjab]], Pakistan. The family is historically associated with the custodianship of the [[Sher Shah Suri Mosque|Sher Shah Suri Mosque (Jamia Masjid Bhera)]], with the founding of [[#Educational institutions|Dar-ul-Uloom Azizia]], a regional Islamic seminary, in 1841, and with religious, educational and political activism in pre- and post-Partition Punjab.<ref name="punjabarch">{{cite web |title=Sher Shah Suri Mosque / Jamia Masjid Bhera |url=https://archaeology.punjab.gov.pk/jame-masjid-bhera |publisher=Directorate General of Archaeology, Government of the Punjab |access-date=3 May 2026}}</ref><ref name="merasargodha">{{cite web |title=Dar-ul-Uloom Azizia Bhera |url=https://merasargodha.com/dar-ul-uloom-azizia-bhera/ |website=Mera Sargodha |access-date=3 May 2026}}</ref>

== History ==
=== Custodianship of Jamia Masjid Bhera ===
The Bugvi family has administered the [[Sher Shah Suri Mosque|Sher Shah Suri Mosque (Jamia Masjid Bhera)]], built in the [[Sur Empire|Sur era]] in 1540 AD, since the nineteenth century.<ref name="punjabarch" /> '''Qazi Ahmed-ud-din Bugvi''' rebuilt the Jamia Masjid in 1858, adding two halls dedicated to Quran and Hadith study and adjacent boarding houses for students.<ref name="punjabarch" /> The mosque was repaired again by '''Qazi Zahoor Ahmed Bugvi''' in 1926.<ref name="punjabarch" />

=== Educational institutions ===
The family established '''Dar-ul-Uloom Azizia''' at the Sher Shah Suri Mosque in 1841 as a centre for the teaching of [[Quran]], [[hadith]] and the religious sciences.<ref name="merasargodha" /><ref name="ihya">{{cite journal |title=The Development of Dar-ul-Uloom Azizia, Bhera and Its Role in the Dissemination of Knowledge to Society (1929–1975 A.D.): A Research Study |url=https://joqs-uok.com/index.php/ihya/article/view/64 |journal=Iḥyāʾ al-ʿUlūm |publisher=Department of Quran o Sunnah, University of Karachi |access-date=3 May 2026}}</ref> The institution operated alongside the family's '''Bugvia Library''', described in regional documentation as approximately two centuries old, and a girls' high school, the '''Bugvia Marryland Girls Model High School Bhera'''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Islamic Educational Institutions Bhera City and Villages |url=http://bheratehsil.blogspot.com/2017/05/islamic-educational-institutions-bhera.html |website=Bhera Tehsil |date=May 2017 |access-date=3 May 2026}}</ref>

The family began publishing the monthly Urdu magazine ''Shams-ul-Islam'' in 1925.<ref name="merasargodha" />

=== Khilafat Movement ===
'''Maulana Zahoor Ahmed Bugvi''' (b.&nbsp;c.&nbsp;1900) was a leading organiser of the [[Khilafat Movement]] in [[Sargodha District|Sargodha district]]. While studying at [[Islamia College, Lahore|Islamia College Lahore]] (intermediate, 1919–21), he founded a Khilafat committee at Bhera and travelled to towns and villages across [[Shahpur District|Shahpur]], [[Khushab District|Khushab]] and the [[Soon Valley|Soon valley]] addressing Khilafat processions and distributing pamphlets in support of the movement.<ref name="gpr">{{cite journal |title=The Emergence of the Khilafat Movement in Sargodha: Beginning of Agitational Politics and Impacts on the Freedom Movement |url=https://www.gprjournal.com/fulltext/the-emergence-of-the-khilafat-movement-in-sargodha-beginning-of-agitational-politics-and-impacts-on-the-freedom-movement |journal=Global Political Review |access-date=3 May 2026}}</ref> He was arrested at [[Sargodha]] on 15 March 1922 and sentenced by an English Summary Court to one-and-a-half years' imprisonment, which he served in [[Jhelum]] and [[Rawalpindi]] jails before his release in late 1923.<ref name="gpr" /> After the formal end of the Khilafat movement he was appointed District Organiser of [[Jamat Tableegh Islam]] Sargodha on the direction of the Central Khilafat Committee of India.<ref name="gpr" />

=== Modern era ===
The family continues to be associated with religious and charitable institutions in Bhera. '''Sahibzada Abrar Ahmed Bugvi''' serves as the [[Khatib]] of the Sher Shah Suri Mosque and as in-charge of Dar-ul-Uloom Azizia.<ref>{{cite web |title=Gates of Bhera |url=https://bhera.org/gates-of-bhera/ |website=Bhera.org |access-date=3 May 2026}}</ref>

In 1998 '''[[Anwaar Ahmed Bugvi|Dr Anwaar Ahmed Bugvi]]''' — formerly Adviser Health (Technical) to the [[Government of Punjab, Pakistan|Government of Punjab]]<ref>{{cite news |title=PMF officials may face axe: Exam scandal |url=https://www.dawn.com/news/156569 |work=[[Dawn (newspaper)|Dawn]] |date=14 September 2005}}</ref> — founded the [[Al-Iftikhar Bugvia Foundation]] (AIBF), a [[non-governmental organization]] registered with the [[Punjab Charity Commission]] (registration no. PB-6976792864708031). The foundation operates programmes in healthcare, education, ration distribution, livelihood support and emergency relief across Bhera, [[Bugga Sharif]] and surrounding villages of Sargodha and [[Jhelum District|Jhelum]] districts.<ref name="aibfabout">{{cite web |title=About AIBF |url=https://aibf.ngo/about |publisher=Al-Iftikhar Bugvia Foundation |access-date=3 May 2026}}</ref>

== Notable members ==
* Qazi Ahmed-ud-din Bugvi — rebuilder of Jamia Masjid Bhera (1858)<ref name="punjabarch" />
* Qazi Zahoor Ahmed Bugvi — restored Jamia Masjid Bhera (1926)<ref name="punjabarch" />
* Maulana Zahoor Ahmed Bugvi — Khilafat Movement organiser; arrested 1922<ref name="gpr" />
* Sahibzada Abrar Ahmed Bugvi — Khatib, Sher Shah Suri Mosque; in-charge, Dar-ul-Uloom Azizia
* [[Anwaar Ahmed Bugvi]] — physician; former Adviser Health (Technical), Punjab; founder of AIBF (1998)
* [[Salman Abubakar Bugvi]] — academic; assistant professor of mechanical engineering, [[University of Lahore]]
* [[Sahibzada Ahmad Muneeb Bugvi]] — author; manager (ORIC), [[University of Health Sciences, Lahore]]
